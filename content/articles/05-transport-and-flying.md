# Article 05 — Transport and Flying: The Single Biggest Lever

**Audience:** general public  
**Reading time:** ~7 minutes (about 3 pages)  
**Stage relevance:** Stage 3–5 drivers; one transatlantic flight ≈ Stage 2 alone

---

## Summary

For most people in high-income countries, transportation is the largest single piece of their carbon footprint — typically 4 to 8 tonnes of CO2e per year, or about 40% of the total. Within transport, the biggest contributors are usually private gasoline cars (about 0.404 kg CO2e per mile) and air travel (about 0.195–0.255 kg CO2e per passenger-mile). A single round-trip transatlantic flight adds about 1.5 tonnes of CO2 — roughly half a year of average US car driving. Public transit, intercity rail, and short-haul electric vehicles can cut per-mile emissions by 50–90% compared with solo driving in a gasoline car.

The good news is that transport emissions are among the easiest to reduce with technology: electric vehicles, heat pumps, and clean electricity grids already exist. The biggest personal changes come from driving less, swapping car trips for walking, biking, transit, or remote work, and limiting air travel — particularly long-haul flights.

---

## 1. Why transport dominates

Modern life in car-dependent regions assumes cheap gasoline and unlimited flying. Both activities release large amounts of CO2 per unit of distance traveled, and both are highly visible on a personal footprint.

*Source:* IPCC AR6 WG3 Ch. 10 (Transport) — public domain — [ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-10](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-10/)

## 2. Car emissions, by type

| Vehicle | kg CO2e / mile | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| Gasoline car (US avg ~22 mpg) | 0.404 | |
| Diesel car | 0.430 | Slightly higher |
| Hybrid (HEV) | ~0.200 | About half |
| Plug-in hybrid (PHEV) | ~0.150–0.250 | Depends on grid |
| Battery EV (US grid mix) | ~0.150 | Falls to ~0.05 on a clean grid |
| Battery EV (renewable grid) | ~0.030 | |
| Motorcycle | 0.180 | |
| E-bike (electricity only) | ~0.020 | |
| Bicycle | 0 | |

A typical US driver logs about 13,500 miles/year, emitting roughly **5.5 t CO2e/yr** from their car alone. Switching to a hybrid cuts that to ~2.7 t; switching to an EV on the current US grid cuts it to ~2.0 t; on a fully renewable grid, ~0.4 t.

*Source:* EPA Emission Factors Hub — public domain — [epa.gov/climateleadership/ghg-emission-factors-hub](https://www.epa.gov/climateleadership/ghg-emission-factors-hub)

## 3. Flying — small numbers of flights, big impact

Air travel concentrates emissions into a small number of trips:

- One round-trip NYC → London: ~1.5 t CO2e
- One round-trip NYC → Los Angeles: ~0.9 t CO2e
- One round-trip London → Sydney: ~3.0 t CO2e
- One round-trip intra-EU (e.g., Paris → Madrid): ~0.3 t CO2e

A single long-haul round trip per year can move a person from Stage 2 to Stage 3. Two long-haul flights per year can move a low emitter into Stage 4.

*Source:* IPCC AR6 WG3 Ch. 9 (Aviation) — public domain — [ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-9](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-9/)

## 4. Public transit and shared mobility

| Mode | kg CO2e / passenger-mile |
|------|--------------------------|
| Subway / metro | ~0.050 |
| Intercity rail | ~0.060 |
| Long-distance coach bus | ~0.050 |
| City bus | ~0.150 |
| Car (avg, single occupant) | 0.404 |
| Ride-hailing (Uber/Lyft avg) | ~0.450 |
| Short-haul flight | 0.255 |

**Key insight:** a full subway or rail car is dramatically cleaner per passenger than a single-occupancy car. Even a city bus beats a single-occupancy gasoline car per passenger-mile.

*Source:* EPA Emission Factors Hub — public domain

## 5. What I can do this week

1. **Replace 1 car commute per day** with walk, bike, transit, or remote work. ~1.0 t CO2e saved per year if it's a 10-mile round trip.
2. **Combine errands** into one weekly trip instead of several. ~0.3 t CO2e saved per year.
3. **Switch to a hybrid or EV** for the next car. 2–4 t CO2e saved per year vs gasoline.
4. **Skip one long-haul flight** per year and take a train instead. ~1.5 t CO2e saved.
5. **Use rail instead of short-haul flights** for trips under 500 miles.
