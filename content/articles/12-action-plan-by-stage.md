# Article 12 — A 12-Month Action Plan by Stage

**Audience:** general public  
**Reading time:** ~7 minutes (about 3 pages)  
**Stage relevance:** direct action map for every stage

---

## Summary

Reducing your carbon footprint does not have to be all at once. A staged, 12-month plan that matches your current stage can move you down one or even two stages while improving comfort and saving money. The plan is tiered: monthly free habits, quarterly low-cost upgrades, and one or two medium-cost home or vehicle upgrades each year. The biggest wins come from transport (replacing car trips, switching to EV), food (replacing beef with beans and cutting waste), and home energy (heat pumps, green electricity, insulation). Systemic action — voting, advocating, organizing — multiplies individual action because policy changes affect everyone.

This article provides a month-by-month action plan tailored to each of the five stages, plus a checklist of medium-cost upgrades to schedule within the year. Following it can move a typical Stage 4 emitter (e.g., US average adult) to Stage 2 within 12 months.

---

## 1. The free daily habits (months 1–12, every day)

- Walk, bike, take transit, or work remotely for at least 1 trip per day.
- Lower heating thermostat 1°C in winter, raise AC 1°C in summer.
- Run dishwasher and washing machine only when full.
- Air-dry clothes when possible.
- Avoid bottled water; use a refillable bottle.
- Plan meals to avoid waste.
- Carry a reusable bag, bottle, and coffee cup.

## 2. The weekly food swaps (every week)

- Replace 1 beef meal per week with beans, lentils, or tofu.
- Cut cheese portions in half on pizza, sandwiches, pasta.
- Try one fully plant-based day per week ("Meatless Monday").
- Buy local, seasonal produce where convenient; otherwise don't worry about it.

**Annual impact:** ~0.5–1.0 t CO2e saved.

## 3. Quarterly low-cost upgrades

| Quarter | Upgrade | Cost | Annual CO2e saved |
|---------|---------|------|-------------------|
| Q1 | Switch to a green-electricity tariff | $0–$10/mo extra | 1–4 t |
| Q2 | Install a smart thermostat | $100–200 | 0.2 t |
| Q3 | Add attic insulation | $1,500–3,000 | 0.5 t |
| Q4 | Replace showerheads and add faucet aerators | $30 | 0.1 t |

## 4. Annual medium-cost upgrades

- **Heat pump water heater** (when old one fails): ~$1,500–2,500 installed, saves ~1 t CO2e/yr.
- **Induction range** (when gas stove fails): ~$1,500–2,500, saves ~0.2 t CO2e/yr.
- **Used EV or hybrid** (when next car purchase): saves 2–4 t CO2e/yr.
- **Heat pump for heating and cooling**: ~$5,000–15,000 installed, saves 2–3 t CO2e/yr.
- **Rooftop solar**: ~$15,000–25,000, saves 1–4 t CO2e/yr; pays back in 5–10 years.

## 5. The 12-month plan by stage

### Stage 1 (0–4 t): hold the line

- Continue daily free habits.
- Add 1 systemic action per quarter (vote, advocate, donate to climate org).
- Skip one long-haul flight per year.

### Stage 2 (4–8 t): daily swaps and food

- Months 1–3: weekly food swaps + 1 trip/day replaced.
- Months 4–6: switch to green-electricity tariff, smart thermostat.
- Months 7–9: plan next-car replacement as EV or hybrid.
- Months 10–12: home insulation audit.

### Stage 3 (8–12 t): home and transport upgrades

- Months 1–3: switch to green-electricity + 1 car-trip swap/day.
- Months 4–6: heat-pump quote for heating/cooling.
- Months 7–9: replace one gas appliance when it fails.
- Months 10–12: install heat-pump water heater.

### Stage 4 (12–16 t): major lifestyle redesign

- Months 1–3: food redesign (3 plant-based days/week, no beef).
- Months 4–6: drop 1 long-haul flight; switch commute to transit/bike.
- Months 7–9: install heat pump + insulate attic.
- Months 10–12: replace next-car with EV; switch to green tariff.

### Stage 5 (16+ t): systemic action + lifestyle reset

- Months 1–3: cut air travel to ≤1 short-haul per year.
- Months 4–6: electrify everything; install rooftop solar.
- Months 7–9: divest from fossil-exposed investments.
- Months 10–12: lead a community action (climate letter, public testimony, mutual-aid retrofit project).

## 6. The systemic multiplier

Whatever your stage, **the highest-leverage actions are collective**: voting for candidates who support carbon pricing, public transit, and electrification; supporting climate-policy advocacy organizations; joining community-solar or mutual-aid retrofit projects. One well-designed policy can cut millions of tonnes of CO2; no individual action can match that scale.

*Source:* IPCC AR6 WG3 Ch. 13 (National and sub-national policies) — public domain — [ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-13](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/chapter/chapter-13/)

## 7. What this looks like in numbers

For a typical Stage 4 US adult (14 t/yr), this 12-month plan can cut footprint to ~6 t/yr — moving them from Stage 4 to Stage 2 in 12 months.
