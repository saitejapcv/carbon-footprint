# Article 01 — Daily Life Activities and Their Carbon Footprint

**Audience:** general public  
**Reading time:** ~8 minutes (about 3 pages)  
**Stage relevance:** foundational reference — applies to all 5 stages

---

## Summary

Every part of daily life — what we drive, what we eat, what we buy, how we heat and cool our homes — releases greenhouse gases. This article lists common activities and their approximate emissions in kilograms of CO2-equivalent (kg CO2e), grouped into Transport, Home Energy, Food, and Goods. The numbers come from the EPA Emission Factors Hub (US public domain) and Poore & Nemecek 2018 (CC BY 4.0). Use this as a quick lookup to understand which daily choices matter most and roughly how much each one moves your annual footprint.

Most people in high-income countries emit somewhere between 12 and 16 tonnes of CO2-equivalent per year, and the breakdown below shows why: a single gasoline car, a beef-heavy diet, and a natural-gas-heated home can each contribute 3–5 tonnes on their own. The biggest single daily lever for most households is transportation, followed by food choices, then home heating and cooling.

## 1. Transport (per year unless noted)

### 1.1 Personal vehicles

| Activity | Per-unit factor | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Drive a gasoline car (avg US, ~22 mpg) | **0.404 kg CO2e / mile** | ~8,000 mi/yr ≈ **3.2 t CO2e** |
| Drive a diesel car | 0.430 kg CO2e / mile | Slightly higher than gasoline |
| Drive a hybrid (e.g., Prius) | ~0.200 kg CO2e / mile | About half of a gasoline car |
| Drive a battery EV (US grid mix) | ~0.150 kg CO2e / mile | Falls to ~0.05 on a clean grid |
| Ride a motorcycle | 0.180 kg CO2e / mile | Smaller engine, less fuel |
| Ride an electric scooter / e-bike | ~0.020 kg CO2e / mile | Includes electricity and materials amortized |

### 1.2 Public transit and shared mobility

| Activity | Per-unit factor | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Take a city bus | 0.150 kg CO2e / mile | Per passenger-mile |
| Take an intercity rail / Amtrak | 0.060 kg CO2e / mile | Roughly 1/7 of a gasoline car |
| Take a subway / metro | 0.050 kg CO2e / mile | Cleanest common motorized option |
| Ride a long-distance coach bus | ~0.050 kg CO2e / mile | Very efficient per passenger |
| Ride a domestic ferry | ~0.190 kg CO2e / mile | Varies widely |
| Ride-hailing (Uber/Lyft, average) | ~0.450 kg CO2e / mile | Worse than driving alone (deadhead miles) |

### 1.3 Air travel

| Activity | Per-unit factor | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Short-haul flight (<1500 mi) | **0.255 kg CO2e / passenger-mile** | Higher climate impact than driving per mile |
| Long-haul flight (>1500 mi) | **0.195 kg CO2e / passenger-mile** | Includes radiative forcing |
| One round-trip NYC → London | ~1.5 t CO2e | About half a year of US car driving |
| One round-trip NYC → Los Angeles | ~0.9 t CO2e | Roughly 3 months of car driving |
| One round-trip SF → Tokyo | ~2.7 t CO2e | More than a year of EU car driving |

## 2. Home Energy (per year)

### 2.1 Electricity

| Activity | Per-unit factor | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Use 1 kWh from US grid average | **0.371 kg CO2e** | Falls to ~0.05 on a clean grid (wind/solar) |
| Run a 100 W LED bulb 4 hrs/day for a year | ~5 kg CO2e | Very small |
| Run an air conditioner 8 hrs/day in summer | ~250–500 kg CO2e / summer | Depends on climate and SEER rating |
| Charge an EV for 12,000 mi of driving | ~1,800 kg CO2e | US grid average; ~600 kg on clean grid |

### 2.2 Heating fuels

| Activity | Per-unit factor | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Burn 1 therm of natural gas | **53.07 kg CO2e** | Avg US home: ~600 therms/yr = **~32 t CO2e for gas heating** is unusual; typical home gas heat ~ **4–6 t CO2e/yr** |
| Burn 1 gallon of fuel oil (heating) | 10.21 kg CO2e | Common in US Northeast |
| Burn 1 gallon of propane | 5.68 kg CO2e | Common in rural US |
| Use district heating (US avg) | 75 kg CO2e / MMBtu | Varies a lot by region |

### 2.3 Home appliances (annual, US household)

## 3. Food (kg CO2e per kg of food, per year of weekly consumption)

| Food | kg CO2e / kg food | Per typical serving |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Beef (herd average) | **60.0** | ~9 kg CO2e per 150 g steak |
| Lamb | 24.0 | ~3.6 kg CO2e per 150 g serving |
| Cheese | 21.0 | ~2.1 kg CO2e per 100 g |
| Chocolate (dark) | 19.0 | ~1.9 kg CO2e per 100 g bar |
| Pork | 7.0 | ~1.0 kg CO2e per 150 g serving |
| Poultry (chicken) | 6.0 | ~0.9 kg CO2e per 150 g serving |
| Farmed fish (avg) | 5.0 | ~0.75 kg CO2e per 150 g |
| Eggs | 4.5 | ~0.36 kg CO2e per 60 g egg |
| Rice | 4.0 | ~0.6 kg CO2e per 150 g cooked |
| Milk | 3.2 | ~0.5 kg CO2e per 250 ml glass |
| Tofu | 3.0 | ~0.45 kg CO2e per 150 g |
| Beans / lentils (dry) | 0.9 | ~0.13 kg CO2e per 150 g cooked |
| Vegetables (avg) | 0.5 | ~0.075 kg CO2e per 150 g |
| Fruits (avg) | 0.5 | ~0.075 kg CO2e per 150 g |

**Annual example — beef-heavy vs plant-leaning diet:**

- 3 servings of beef per week, 100 g each: ~140 kg CO2e/yr from beef alone.
- 1 serving of beef per week, rest plant-based: ~45 kg CO2e/yr from beef.

## 5. Putting It Together — Typical US Adult Annual Footprint

| Category | Typical range | Share of total |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Transport (car + flights) | 4–8 t CO2e | ~40% |
| Home energy | 2–5 t CO2e | ~20% |
| Food | 2–4 t CO2e | ~20% |
| Goods and services | 2–4 t CO2e | ~20% |

**Total:** ~10–21 t CO2e per year, with the median around 14 t. Compare to the **2.5 t 1.5°C-aligned target** and the **4.7 t global average**.
