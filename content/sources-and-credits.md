# Sources & Credits — Copyright Compliance Guide

**Audience:** site maintainer / educator  
**Purpose:** document every source used in this content pack, its license, and how to credit it correctly so the site stays 100% legal on a free, non-commercial educational site.

---

## 1. License Hierarchy (followed everywhere in this pack)

In this strict order of preference:

1. **US / global public domain** — no credit required, but we list the source for transparency.
2. **CC0** — no credit required, but we list the source for transparency.
3. **CC BY 4.0** — one-line credit required. This is the highest-licensed material we use.
4. **Anything else (CC BY-SA, CC BY-NC, paywalled, commercial)** — **excluded entirely.**

## 3. How to Credit CC BY 4.0 Sources Correctly

**Required elements for every CC BY 4.0 credit line:**

1. **Title** of the work
2. **Author(s)** of the work
3. **Source / publisher** (e.g., *Science*, Our World in Data)
4. **License** (CC BY 4.0)
5. **URL** to the original

**Where to put credits on the site:**

- A dedicated "Sources & Credits" page reachable from the footer of every page.
- For inline references, an unobtrusive footnote or hover tooltip containing the same five elements.
- Keep the URL live (clickable) so credit is machine-readable.

**What you may do without asking:**

- Copy, redistribute, adapt, remix, translate.
- Use commercially (this site is non-commercial but CC BY allows commercial too).

**What you must not do:**

- Apply additional restrictions (no DRM, no "all rights reserved" wrappers).
- Sublicense under different terms.
- Misattribute authorship.

**What is excluded:**

- CC BY-SA 4.0 (share-alike clause complicates republishing) — **not used here.**
- CC BY-NC 4.0 (non-commercial clause restricts derivatives) — **not used here.**
- Anything behind a paywall, anything commercial-copyrighted, anything from fossil-fuel-industry or offset-company marketing — **not used here.**
