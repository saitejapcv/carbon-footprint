ARTICLE 01 — REFERENCE

Daily Life Activities & Carbon Footprint

Every part of daily life — what we drive, what we eat, what we buy, how we heat and cool our homes — releases greenhouse gases. Transportation is the largest single lever, followed by home energy and food choices.

ARTICLE 02 — FOUNDATIONAL

What Is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint represents the total greenhouse-gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by an individual or product. It includes carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and refrigerants converted to CO₂e.

GLOBAL AVERAGE 4.7 t CO₂e/yr
1.5°C TARGET 2.5 t CO₂e/yr
ARTICLE 03 — SCIENCE

Climate Change 101: Why It Matters

Human activities have thickened the greenhouse-gas blanket by 50% since pre-industrial times, warming the planet by 1.1°C. Reaching the Paris Agreement target requires cutting global emissions in half by 2030.
